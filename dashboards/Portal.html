Client Portal Blueprint for Eric’s Coaching Practice

A two-layer architecture that delivers a complete coaching client portal, using Go High Level (GHL) as the operational engine and an optional polished front-end for a premium client experience.

1. Purpose & Context

This document outlines a proposed approach to designing and implementing a complete client portal for Eric’s coaching clients. The goal is to:

  • Increase client engagement and accountability throughout the coaching journey.
  • Centralize communication, resources, and progress tracking in one place.
  • Leverage Eric’s existing investment in Go High Level (GHL) as the operational backend.
  • Provide a clear path to a more polished, branded portal experience over time.

Note: This is a planning document. It can be adapted into a formal proposal or scope of work if Eric chooses to move forward.

2. What Coaching Clients Need from a Portal

Across coaching businesses (executive, business, and personal development), the most effective portals tend to support a similar lifecycle: onboard → plan → execute → track → adjust → sustain. For Eric’s practice, we can focus on the following core capabilities:

2.1 Onboarding & Program Orientation

  • Welcome experience and program overview.
  • Intake forms and baseline assessments.
  • Clarity on expectations, cadence, and outcomes.

2.2 Goals & Outcomes Tracking

  • Documented goals (business, personal, or leadership outcomes).
  • Milestones and supporting habits or projects.
  • Simple visual indicator of “where I was vs. where I am now.”

2.3 Session Notes & Action Items

  • Session summaries available after each call.
  • Clear action items with due dates and status.
  • Ability for the client to check off completed items between sessions.

2.4 Resources Library

  • Templates, PDFs, worksheets, and videos.
  • Organized by topic or phase of the engagement.
  • Easy for clients to find what they need without emailing Eric.

2.5 Weekly Check-ins & Reflections

  • Short weekly check-in form (wins, challenges, focus).
  • Optional journaling prompts to track mindset shifts.
  • Trend view over time to support deeper coaching conversations.

2.6 Scheduling & Logistics

  • Self-service scheduling and rescheduling of coaching sessions.
  • Clear view of upcoming sessions.
  • Email/SMS reminders to reduce no-shows.

2.7 Billing & Account Management

  • View invoices and payment history.
  • Manage subscriptions (where applicable).
  • Update payment methods without back-and-forth emails.
Intentionally excluded: group/community features (forums, peer chat, group feeds). This allows us to aim for ~100% coverage of the client experience using Go High Level as the core engine.

3. Overall Architecture

3.1 Backend: Go High Level (GHL)

GHL will serve as the operational backbone of the portal. It already supports the main primitives needed for a coaching practice:

  • Contacts and custom fields for client data and goals.
  • Pipelines and stages to model the coaching journey.
  • Calendars and booking links for sessions.
  • Forms for intake, check-ins, and reflections.
  • Membership areas for resources and structured content.
  • Automations for reminders, follow-ups, and task creation.
  • Invoices, subscriptions, and payments.

The initial implementation can live entirely inside GHL, providing a “Phase 1” portal without introducing new platforms.

3.2 Optional Front-End Portal

For a more polished client experience, we can later introduce a custom front-end portal that:

  • Provides a clean, branded dashboard for clients.
  • Surfaces key data from GHL (goals, progress, tasks, sessions, billing) via API.
  • Maintains GHL as the single source of truth and automation engine.

This two-layer approach gives Eric a “best of both worlds” model: operational simplicity with GHL, and high-end UX when he’s ready for it.

Phase 1: GHL-Only Portal Phase 2: Branded Front-End (Optional)

4. Phase 1 – Complete Portal Within Go High Level

Phase 1 focuses on building a complete, fully functional client portal using only GHL features. This minimizes complexity and leverages tools Eric already uses.

4.1 Core Components

1

Onboarding & Intake

  • Dedicated onboarding funnel for new coaching clients.
  • Intake form capturing goals, context, and constraints.
  • Automated welcome email with portal access details.
  • Optional pre-work content delivered via membership area.
2

Goals & Progress Tracking

  • Custom fields in GHL for key goals and metrics.
  • Pipeline or tags representing major milestones achieved.
  • Simple visual cues inside the portal (e.g., “Current Focus”, “This Month’s Target”).
3

Session Notes & Action Items

  • Session notes stored per client, accessible through the portal (e.g., via membership content or custom fields surfaced).
  • Action items managed as tasks or structured checklists per client.
  • Automated reminders before/after sessions to confirm and review action items.
4

Resources Library

  • Use GHL membership areas to organize resources by topic or program.
  • Gate advanced content based on client status or plan level.
  • Ensure a single “Resources” area is visible inside the client portal.
5

Weekly Check-ins & Journaling

  • Short weekly check-in form (wins, challenges, next focus).
  • Automations to send the form link at a consistent time each week.
  • Field updates or notes created from submissions to support tracking and review.
6

Scheduling

  • GHL calendars exposed inside the portal for session booking.
  • Automated reminders and follow-up campaigns tied to calendar events.
7

Billing & Payment

  • Use GHL’s built-in payment features for subscriptions or packages.
  • Allow clients to view invoices and payment history within the portal.
  • Automated notifications for upcoming renewals or failed payments.

4.2 Outcomes of Phase 1

  • A single place for Eric’s clients to manage their coaching experience.
  • Increased accountability and follow-through between sessions.
  • Reduced administrative overhead for Eric and his team.
  • Ability to test and refine the portal structure before investing in a custom front end.

5. Phase 2 – Optional Branded Front-End Portal

Once Phase 1 is stable and Eric has clear proof of value, we can introduce a custom front-end portal to elevate the visual experience while continuing to use GHL as the backend.

5.1 Front-End Experience

  • Custom-branded login and dashboard for clients.
  • Navigation tailored to Eric’s methodology (e.g., “Start Here”, “Goals”, “Actions”, “Sessions”, “Resources”, “Billing”).
  • Cleaner visualizations for goals, progress, and session history.

5.2 Integration with GHL

The front-end would not replace GHL; it would sit on top of it:

  • GHL remains the source of truth for contacts, goals, sessions, tasks, and billing.
  • The portal reads and writes data via GHL’s API and webhooks.
  • Forms embedded in the portal submit directly into GHL workflows.

5.3 Benefits of This Two-Layer Model

  • High-end, SaaS-like client experience without rebuilding backend logic.
  • Flexibility to evolve the UI over time as Eric’s practice grows or shifts.
  • Clear separation between operations (GHL) and presentation (custom portal).

6. Suggested Engagement Structure (If Eric Proceeds)

6.1 Phase 1 – GHL Portal Build

  • Clarify Eric’s ideal client journey and coaching offer structure.
  • Design the portal information architecture using existing GHL assets where possible.
  • Configure portals, membership areas, forms, calendars, and automations in GHL.
  • Test with a small group of clients and refine based on feedback.

6.2 Phase 2 – Optional Front-End Portal

  • Define UX requirements and visual style for a custom portal.
  • Map GHL data structures to front-end components.
  • Implement and test integration with GHL (API/webhooks/forms).
  • Gradually onboard clients from the GHL-native view to the new front end.
Flexibility: Phase 2 is optional and can be introduced only if/when Eric wants to elevate the visual experience beyond what GHL offers natively. Phase 1 alone provides a complete, functional portal.

7. Summary

This blueprint gives Eric a path to a complete coaching client portal that:

  • Centralizes onboarding, goals, action items, resources, check-ins, scheduling, and billing.
  • Maximizes the value of his existing Go High Level investment.
  • Allows for a clean “Phase 1 now, Phase 2 later” approach to design and development.
  • Improves client accountability, perceived value, and retention.
