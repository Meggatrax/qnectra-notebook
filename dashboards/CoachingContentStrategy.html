Coaching ICP — 90-Day LinkedIn Content Strategy

Goal: post 1x per week for 12 weeks. Theme: turn onboarding + delivery into a system so growth doesn’t create more manual work.

Cadence: Weekly
Pillars: CX Ops · Delivery/Retention · Revenue Systems
CTAs: PORTAL · ONBOARD · SYSTEMS

Content Pillars (rotate weekly)

This keeps your feed coherent while still feeling varied. If you ever feel stuck: pick a pillar → pick a common pain → ship one practical move.

1) Client Experience Ops

Onboarding · Portal · Follow-ups

Reduce “manual follow-up tax.” Make the first 7 days feel premium, clear, and self-serve.

2) Delivery & Retention Ops

Fulfillment · Progress · Renewals

Stop delivering from memory. Build light structure so results feel trackable and renewals feel natural.

3) Revenue Systems Ops

Pipeline · Attribution · Automations

Turn “sales vibes” into a flow: lead capture → pipeline stages → intake → upsell, without adding chaos.

12-Week Plan (1 post/week)

Each week includes: angle + a few practical moves + suggested CTA. Click to expand a week.

Week 1

Your onboarding is part of your offer.

Month 1 · Client Experience Ops

▾
Angle: The first 7 days decide confidence, support load, and retention.
Practical moves:
  • Define “Day 0 → Day 7” as a checklist (payment, scheduling, access, expectations, next steps).
  • One home base: “Start Here” page with 3 links max.
  • Set communication rules once (response times, where to ask what).
CTA: Comment ONBOARD and I’ll send a 10-minute onboarding audit checklist.
Week 2

The “manual follow-up tax” (and how to kill it).

Month 1 · Client Experience Ops

▾
Angle: Most operators don’t notice how much time is lost chasing basics.
Practical moves:
  • List your top 5 follow-ups. Automate only those first.
  • Use a single intake form + conditional routing (don’t split forms unless necessary).
  • Create “nudge ladder”: reminder #1, #2, then a human check-in.
CTA: Comment PORTAL and I’ll share the portal structure that eliminates most follow-ups.
Week 3

Client portals aren’t a feature. They’re a promise.

Month 1 · Client Experience Ops

▾
Angle: A good portal says: “you’re in the right place, and you know what happens next.”
Practical moves:
  • Portal must answer 3 questions: where am I, what’s next, how do I get help?
  • Keep navigation brutally simple: Home, Resources, Progress, Support.
  • Reduce “scroll chaos” with a short weekly cadence card.
CTA: Comment PORTAL for a “Client Portal Blueprint” outline.
Week 4

The 5 onboarding assets every coaching business needs.

Month 1 · Client Experience Ops

▾
Angle: Premium onboarding isn’t “more content.” It’s clearer structure.
Practical moves:
  • Welcome page (start here).
  • Expectations doc (what success requires).
  • Kickoff workflow (steps + timing).
  • Communication rules (where/how).
  • Progress tracker (simple + visible).
CTA: Comment ONBOARD and I’ll send a 1-page onboarding asset checklist.
Week 5

Stop delivering from memory.

Month 2 · Delivery & Retention Ops

▾
Angle: Memory-based delivery creates inconsistency and founder bottlenecks.
Practical moves:
  • Write your “minimum viable SOP”: inputs → steps → outputs.
  • Define what “done” looks like for each stage of the client journey.
  • Turn repeated advice into a reusable asset (doc/video).
CTA: DM SOP and I’ll share a simple SOP outline for coaching delivery.
Week 6

Progress tracking is retention insurance.

Month 2 · Delivery & Retention Ops

▾
Angle: Clients churn when they can’t see the arc of progress.
Practical moves:
  • One metric per week: effort, consistency, or milestone.
  • Monthly recap: “where you started → what changed → what’s next.”
  • Use a simple tracker before buying new tools.
CTA: Comment TRACK and I’ll share a simple progress-tracker concept.
Week 7

Support requests are a systems signal.

Month 2 · Delivery & Retention Ops

▾
Angle: Repeated questions are not a “client problem.” They’re an ops map.
Practical moves:
  • Tag every question for 2 weeks (access, scheduling, expectations, tools, mindset, deliverables).
  • Fix the top 2 categories with one new asset each (FAQ, checklist, walkthrough).
  • Add “Where to ask what” inside the portal.
CTA: Comment SUPPORT for a request taxonomy you can copy.
Week 8

The renewal is decided before the last session.

Month 2 · Delivery & Retention Ops

▾
Angle: Renewals follow clarity: results + next horizon + a simple plan.
Practical moves:
  • Start value recaps at week 3–4 (not at the end).
  • Build a “next horizon” page: goals, obstacles, plan, options.
  • Make renewal a workflow, not an awkward conversation.
CTA: DM RENEW for a simple renewal cadence you can run monthly.
Week 9

Your pipeline isn’t “sales.” It’s a manufacturing line.

Month 3 · Revenue Systems Ops

▾
Angle: A pipeline is only real when each stage has a clear exit criteria.
Practical moves:
  • Define 5–7 stages max (lead → convo → proposal → decision → paid → onboarded).
  • Write one sentence: “To move forward, we must have ___.”
  • Stop forecasting from hope. Forecast from stage math.
CTA: Comment PIPELINE and I’ll share a stage map for coaching offers.
Week 10

Attribution doesn’t need to be perfect—just consistent.

Month 3 · Revenue Systems Ops

▾
Angle: Consistency beats complexity. Track what actually informs decisions.
Practical moves:
  • Pick 5 sources you care about (LinkedIn, referral, email, webinar, ads).
  • Add one required field to intake: “Where did you find me?”
  • Review monthly: what produced qualified conversations?
CTA: DM ATTRIB for a minimal attribution setup you can implement in an hour.
Week 11

Automations should protect relationships, not replace them.

Month 3 · Revenue Systems Ops

▾
Angle: Automation is best when it removes friction, not warmth.
Practical moves:
  • Automate reminders, routing, and status updates.
  • Keep moments of care human: celebration, hard truths, renewal decisions.
  • Use “if stuck → human check-in” triggers.
CTA: Comment AUTOMATE for a “good automation list” for coaching ops.
Week 12

The Client Journey Map (your next 90 days in one page).

Month 3 · Revenue Systems Ops

▾
Angle: Your business becomes calm when the journey is visible end-to-end.
Practical moves:
  • Map: lead → pipeline → close → intake → onboarding → delivery → outcomes → renewal/referral.
  • Pick the top 2 friction points; fix those first.
  • Turn the map into your Q1/Q2 roadmap.
CTA: DM SYSTEMS for a quick teardown of your client journey and the first 3 fixes.
